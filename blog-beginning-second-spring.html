The Beginning of My Second Spring: How Peri-Menopause Became My Unexpected Rebirth

I never expected peri-menopause to be the catalyst for my most profound transformation.

But here I am, two years into this journey, and I can honestly say it's been the most empowering period of my life.

Let me take you back to where it all began.

The Wake-Up Call

It started with subtle changes—the kind that make you question if you're imagining things. My periods, which had been like clockwork for years, began arriving a few days late. Then came the 3 AM wake-ups, not from anxiety or racing thoughts, but from something deeper, something hormonal.

I was 42, and I knew what this meant. But instead of dreading it, something inside me shifted. I realized I had a choice: I could see this as the beginning of the end, or I could see it as the beginning of something entirely new.

I chose the latter.

The Research Phase

As someone who's always been curious about health and wellness, I dove deep into understanding what was happening to my body. I read everything I could find about peri-menopause, from medical journals to ancient wisdom traditions.

What I discovered was fascinating: while Western medicine often frames menopause as a decline, many cultures around the world see it as a time of renewal and wisdom. In Chinese medicine, it's called the "Second Spring"—a time when women step into their power and purpose.

This reframe changed everything for me.

Embracing Ancient Wisdom

I began incorporating practices from Traditional Chinese Medicine and Ayurveda into my daily routine. Acupuncture became a regular part of my self-care. I started paying attention to my body's rhythms in ways I never had before.

I learned that peri-menopause isn't just about hormones declining—it's about them fluctuating wildly, creating a unique opportunity for healing and transformation. The key is learning to work with these fluctuations, not against them.

The Physical Changes

Yes, there were challenges. The hot flashes that would hit me at the most inconvenient times. The brain fog that made me forget where I put my keys (again). The mood swings that could turn a minor inconvenience into a major crisis.

But I also noticed positive changes. My intuition became sharper. I had more energy for the things that truly mattered to me. I felt more connected to my body and its wisdom than ever before.

The Emotional Journey

This is where the real transformation happened. Peri-menopause forced me to confront parts of myself I'd been avoiding. Old wounds resurfaced, demanding to be healed. Patterns that no longer served me became impossible to ignore.

It wasn't always comfortable, but it was necessary. I began therapy, started journaling, and made space for emotions I'd been pushing down for decades. I learned that healing isn't linear, and that's okay.

Finding My Voice

As I navigated this transition, I realized how little support exists for women going through peri-menopause. The medical system often dismisses our symptoms or offers one-size-fits-all solutions that don't address the complexity of this life stage.

I started sharing my journey on social media, and the response was overwhelming. Women were hungry for information, for community, for someone who understood what they were experiencing.

This is how SecondSpring was born—from my own need for better resources and my desire to help other women navigate this transition with grace and empowerment.

The Spiritual Awakening

Peri-menopause has been, above all, a spiritual journey. It's forced me to slow down, to listen to my body, to honor my needs in ways I never had before. I've learned to say no without guilt, to prioritize my well-being, to trust my inner wisdom.

I've discovered that this stage of life isn't about becoming less—it's about becoming more of who I truly am. It's about shedding the expectations and roles that no longer fit and stepping into my authentic power.

What I've Learned

Two years into this journey, here's what I know for sure:

Peri-menopause is not a medical condition to be fixed—it's a natural transition to be honored.

Our bodies are incredibly wise, and when we listen to them, they guide us toward healing.

Ancient wisdom traditions have so much to offer modern women navigating this transition.

Community and support make all the difference.

This stage of life is a gift, not a curse.

Looking Forward

As I continue to navigate this journey, I'm excited about what's to come. I'm building a platform that will help other women access the resources and support I wish I'd had when I started this journey.

I'm also excited about the next phase of my life. Peri-menopause has taught me that change is not something to fear—it's something to embrace. It's shown me that I'm stronger, more resilient, and more capable than I ever imagined.

My Second Spring is just beginning, and I can't wait to see what blooms.

For You

If you're reading this and you're in the midst of your own peri-menopause journey, know this: you're not alone, and you're not broken. This transition is a natural part of being a woman, and it can be one of the most transformative periods of your life.

Trust your body. Honor your needs. Seek support when you need it. And remember: this is your Second Spring, your time to bloom into the woman you were always meant to be.

Welcome to your rebirth.
